Frequently Asked Questions

B.Pharm (Bachelor of Pharmacy) and D.Pharm (Diploma in Pharmacy) programs differ in duration, depth of study, and career outcomes. Our B.Pharm program is a comprehensive four-year undergraduate degree, while the D.Pharm program is a two-year diploma with a focus on practical skills. The B.Pharm degree opens avenues for advanced research and managerial roles, whereas D.Pharm prepares students for entry-level positions in the pharmaceutical industry.

Our pharmacy programs emphasize hands-on learning. Students engage in laboratory work, pharmaceutical manufacturing simulations, and internships with industry partners. These practical experiences ensure a well-rounded education, equipping graduates with the skills and confidence needed in real-world pharmacy settings.

We are committed to helping our pharmacy graduates succeed in their careers. Our career support services include resume building, interview preparation, and job placement assistance. We also facilitate industry interactions, networking events, and internships to connect students with potential employers in the pharmaceutical sector.

We prioritize staying at the forefront of pharmaceutical advancements. Our faculty members actively engage in research, attend conferences, and collaborate with industry professionals to bring the latest knowledge to the classroom. Additionally, our curriculum is regularly updated to reflect the evolving landscape of pharmaceutical sciences, ensuring that students receive education aligned with current industry trends and practices.div>
